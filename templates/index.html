    Renewable energy is energy that comes from natural resources such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, plants, waves and geothermal heat. These energy resources are renewable, meaning they are naturally replenished. Renewable energy has the potential to provide 80 percent of U.S. electricity by 2050.


    The major types of renewable energy sources: Biomass, Hydropower, Geothermal, Wind and Solar.

    Every year, much of the energy the U.S. consumes is wasted through transmission, heat loss and inefficient technology, costing American families and businesses money, and leading to increased carbon pollution.

    States have adopted a number of policies to support renewable energy technologies. Each state has its own Renewable Portfolio Standards (RPS), which require that a specified percentage of the electricity that utilities sell comes from renewable resources. States have created these standards to diversify their energy resources, promote domestic energy production and encourage economic development.
